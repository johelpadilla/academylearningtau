# Domain: Ecosystem Ecology — Lake Eutrophication

## 1. Context

Shallow lakes can shift from a **clear oligotrophic** state to a **turbid eutrophic** state (Scheffer et al.). Series of chlorophyll-a, nutrients, and dissolved oxygen capture this transition on monthly/annual scales (e.g., NTL LTER, Lake Mendota).

## 2. Classical EWS: Origin and Limits

This domain is the **historical home** of CSD-based EWS. They work in many models and in some lakes. They fail or are debated when:

- the forcing is strongly seasonal,
- there is human management (nutrients),
- the observation is multivariate and the transition belongs to the **food web**, not just a single indicator.

## 3. Value of τ_s + RECD

- Same relational language as in physiology: the lake "coordinates" nutrients–Chl-a–DO.
- RECD offers a clock of **ecological reorganization events**.
- Allows **methodological transfer** between heart ↔ lake (the platform's unifying thesis).
